# Diploma & Survival Guide (Based on Maria's Advice)

This guide aggregates advice regarding the final year, specifically focusing on the diploma process, course specifics, and survival tips.

## üìÖ Diploma Timeline & Process

### 1. Topic & Supervisor Selection (October - November)
*   **Deadline:** Mid-to-late November.
*   **Advice:**
    *   **Do not** take the default topics offered by the department unless you want a strict supervisor and a boring topic.
    *   **Strategy:** Look at the archive of past theses (available on the university website/Mano). Find supervisors who have supervised topics similar to what you want (e.g., Software vs. Hardware).
    *   **Approach:** Email potential supervisors directly. "Hello, I have this idea..."
    *   **Topic Type:** You can choose **Research** or **Development**. Most students (and this guide) focus on **Development**.
    *   **Software vs. Hardware:** Computer Engineering often pushes for hardware. If you want pure software, you need to fight for it or find a supervisor who supports it (check archives).

### 2. Objectives & Requirements (December)
*   **Deadline:** End of December.
*   **Deliverable:** A formal document approved by the department.
*   **Importance:** **Critical**. This defines your grading criteria.
    *   If you list a requirement, you *must* show it in your final system.
    *   **Do not** generate this blindly with ChatGPT.
    *   Hard to change later (requires bureaucracy).

### 3. BD1 - Bachelor Defense 1 (January)
*   **Timing:** End of 7th Semester (Exam Session).
*   **Format:** Pass/Fail. Presentation (usually scrolling through your PDF).
*   **Content (~10-15 pages):**
    *   Title Page.
    *   Introduction.
    *   Review of Similar Systems.
    *   General Architecture + Diagram.
    *   Objectives & Requirements.
*   **Reviewer:** The professor leading the "Bachelor Graduation Thesis 1" course.

### 4. Development Phase (February - April)
*   **Advice:**
    *   Keep your supervisor updated **every 2 weeks**.
    *   **Demo Presentations:** Start around March/April (every 2 weeks). You must show progress (code/implementation).
    *   **Warning:** Don't leave the technical part for April.

### 5. "IoT Systems" / Coursework Defense (April 30th)
*   **What is it?** A course in the 8th semester that is essentially a "mini-defense" of your diploma's practical part.
*   **Deliverable:** A coursework report (~20-25 pages) based on your diploma's practical work (excluding the heavy theory/intro).
*   **Defense:** Presentation of the practical implementation.

### 6. BD2 - Bachelor Defense 2 (Early May)
*   **Timing:** ~May 9th.
*   **Status:** ~70-80% of work should be done.
*   **Content (~40-45 pages):**
    *   Everything from BD1 + Theory + Implementation details.
    *   Technical part should be mostly working.
*   **Format:** 7-minute presentation.
*   **Reviewers:** Your reviewer + potentially others (e.g., Darius).

### 7. Final Submission & Defense (Late May - Early June)
*   **Submission:** ~May 30th (5 working days before defense).
    *   Upload ZIP to Mano VGTU.
*   **Reviews:**
    1.  **Supervisor Review:** Usually positive.
    2.  **Reviewer (Recenzentas) Review:** Critical. Contains a grade and **2-3 mandatory questions**.
*   **Defense (BD3):** ~June 6th.
    *   **Presentation:** 7 minutes. Strict timing.
    *   **Q&A:** First, answer the mandatory questions from the Reviewer's review. Then, questions from the commission.

## üìù Diploma Structure & Formatting

**Standard Structure:**
1.  **Introduction** (3-5 pages).
2.  **Review of Similar Systems** (End this section with a **Comparison Table**).
3.  **System Architecture and Design** (Diagrams + High-level explanation).
4.  **System Development** (Implementation details).
5.  **Testing**.
6.  **Conclusions** (**Must contain numbers/metrics** - e.g., "System improved efficiency by 20%").
7.  **References**.
8.  **Appendices**.

**Formatting Rules:**
*   **New Pages:** Every Chapter (1., 2.) starts on a new page. Subchapters (1.1) do not.
*   **Images:** No Chapter or Subchapter should *end* with an image.
*   **Captions:** Check "Figure X" and "Table Y" numbering carefully.
*   **Sources:** If an image is not yours, add the source.
*   **Abstract (Annotation):** Generated on Mano VGTU at the very end. Must include a specific sentence counting pages, figures, and tables.

**Tools:**
*   **ZeroGPT:** To check for AI-generated text (keep it low).
*   **WriteHuman:** To help rephrase (use with caution for technical text).

## üìö Course-Specific Advice (7th Semester)

### üêß Linux (Statkus)
*   **Difficulty:** Easy / Passable.
*   **Labs:** Can often be done with ChatGPT/Instructions.
*   **Attendance:** Not strict.
*   **Assessment:** Midterm/Exam involves practical tasks + theory.

### ü§ñ Microcontrollers
*   **Lecturer:** Strict. Exam is a Moodle test (multiple choice), hard to cheat but possible (Alt-Tab skills).
*   **Labs (Practice):**
    *   **Professor:** Hardware enthusiast, kind.
    *   **Attendance:** **Mandatory** (often Friday 8am).
    *   **Content:** Oscilloscopes, boards, wires. Code is often provided.
    *   **Strategy:** Show up, do the work (or pretend to), submit reports.

### üß† AI (Artificial Intelligence)
*   **Lecturer:** Lectures considered useless by many.
*   **Labs:**
    *   Interesting but time-consuming.
    *   4-5 labs (training models, finding datasets).
    *   **Advice:** Start early (2 weeks given). ChatGPT writes good code for this.
*   **Exam:** Moodle test, professor watches closely.

### üì° Telecommunications (Arturas)
*   **Professor:** Arturas (The Dean). Highly recommended ("Golden man").
*   **Labs:**
    *   **Hard work.** Working with routers, switches, network equipment.
    *   **Attendance:** Mandatory.
    *   **Tips:** Follow his detailed instructions. Work fast. Don't copy reports 1-to-1.
    *   **Packet Capture:** You may need to capture packets on multiple PCs (break internet connection to capture local traffic, or take photos of screens).
*   **Exam:** Moodle test. Fair questions.

### üì± Smart Devices (Thomas/Kuzia)
*   **Professor:** Chill, prefers Remote/Zoom.
*   **Content:** Python, React Native, Android Studio.
*   **Biggest Challenge:** **Environment Setup**. Installing all dependencies is the hardest part. Do this early.
*   **Assessment:** Project-based. Show your app running via Zoom.
*   **Exam:** Project presentation.

## üí° Internship

*   **Report:** Generic report describing your "tasks".
*   **Deadline:** Usually documents needed by mid-November (for the semester start) or July (for summer practice).
